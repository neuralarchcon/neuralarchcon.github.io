#### Detailed description

On each trial, participants performed two consecutive tasks: a motor task followed by a perceptual detection task with subjective and objective ratings.

Motor task. Each trial began with a directional arrow cue indicating which button participants should press and hold. The cue could indicate the left arrow key, the right arrow key, a downward (neutral) arrow key, or no cue at all (blank screen), meaning no key press was required. Participants held down the indicated key until the target stimulus appeared on screen.

Perceptual task. The target stimulus was a brief, faint noise patch that either contained a subthreshold grating or consisted of noise alone. As soon as participants detected the flash of the noise patch, they released the held key (if applicable) and provided two sequential ratings. First, they gave a subjective report using the Perceptual Awareness Scale (PAS): (1) no experience of the grating, (2) a brief glimpse, (3) an almost clear experience, or (4) a completely clear experience. Second, they made an objective forced-choice judgment about whether a grating was actually present or absent. Participants were explicitly instructed to base their PAS rating purely on their subjective experience, without concern for accuracy, and to reserve the objective judgment for the second response.

Calibration and experimental blocks. The session began with a staircase block (128 trials) that adjusted the signal-to-noise ratio of the grating to approximate each participant's 50% detection threshold. This was followed by four experimental blocks (256 trials total), in which participants performed the full dual-task procedure. The entire session lasted approximately 30 minutes.

#### Conditions and number of trials

Total of 5 blocks (384 trials): 1 staircase (128 trials) + 4 experimental (256 trials).
Staircase adjusts contrast to ~75% threshold.

2×2×4 factorial design: stimulus presence (present/absent) × orientation (left/right) × cue type (arrow left/right, neutral rectangle, no cue).
* 4 conditions for the motor task (left, right ,neutral, no cue; 60 trials per condition)
* 2 conditions for the perception task (target present/absent; 130 trials per condition)
