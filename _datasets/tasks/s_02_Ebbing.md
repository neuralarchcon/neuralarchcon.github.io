#### Detailed description

This task measures individual susceptibility to the Ebbinghaus size illusion using an adaptive psychophysical procedure, following the paradigm of Song et al. (2017).

On each trial, two central white circles are presented sequentially in randomized order. In the illusion condition, one of the circles is surrounded by seven large white inducer circles (2° diameter), creating the classic Ebbinghaus configuration. In the control condition, neither circle has surrounding inducers. In both conditions, participants judge which of the two central circles appeared larger (2AFC).

Adaptive staircase. The physical size difference between the two central circles is controlled trial-by-trial using a Palamedes staircase, converging on the point of subjective equality. Comparing thresholds between the illusion and control conditions yields a measure of each participant's illusion magnitude.
