#### Detailed description

This task investigates whether overt motor responses carry information that benefits metacognitive judgments, above and beyond the strength of the perceptual signal itself. Participants viewed drifting gratings and made temporal-summation judgments, with motor engagement manipulated across conditions.

Stimuli. On each 5-second trial, two colored vertical gratings appeared in alternation across four consecutive intervals. The gratings were red (drifting rightward) and green (drifting leftward), formed by a sine-wave function (0.27 cycles/°, drift speed 15°/s), drawn inside an 8° square presented at fixation.

The design crossed two factors — continuous report (CR+/CR−) and first-order response (R+/R−) — yielding four conditions. In CR+ trials, participants pressed arrow keys with their right hand to continuously track which stimulus was currently on screen. In CR− trials, no keys were pressed during stimulus presentation. After stimulus offset, participants performed a temporal-summation judgment: indicating which of the two motion directions had been presented for a longer total duration across the trial. In R+ trials this judgment was given overtly via key press; in R− trials the decision was covert and participants proceeded directly to the confidence rating. On any given trial, participants were not informed beforehand whether an overt first-order response would be required. All trials ended with a confidence rating on a vertical visual analog scale (endpoints: "very sure" / "very unsure"), adjusted with two keys.

Calibration. The duration difference between the two stimuli was controlled by two independent 1-up/2-down staircases (one for CR+ and one for CR− trials, updated on R+ trials only). Two 25-trial pre-experiment staircases (without confidence ratings) established individual starting points.

Procedure. After the staircases, each participant completed 240 trials (60 per condition), with trial types interleaved in randomized order. Trials were self-paced. The experiment took approximately 50 minutes.

#### Number of participants (raw)

Krakow: 286 (260 complete, 26 incomplete)

#### Conditions and number of trials

2 conditions (Active hand movements, Visual replay). Per condition: 
* Training (10 trials), 
* Staircase (40 trials), 
* VAS training (10 trials), 
* Main experiment (4 blocks × 50 trials = 200 trials). 
Total: 260 trials per condition.
