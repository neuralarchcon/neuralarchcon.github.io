#### Detailed description
This task aims to test whether expectations can generate illusory percepts of physically absent stimuli under conditions of diverted attention. This paradigm design is a replication of the experiment 2a described by Aru, Tulver & Bachmann (2018).
Stimuli. On each trial, two face photographs appear simultaneously to the left and right of fixation (1.7° each, low contrast). Faces are drawn from a pool of 32 identities (16 male, 16 female) and can be presented upright or inverted. A faint Landolt C square (0.5°, very low contrast) may appear centrally between the faces, oriented in one of four directions (up, right, down, left). Each trial begins with a fixation cross (jittered 500–1000 ms), followed by the stimulus display for 100 ms and a 500 ms blank before the response screen.
Dual-task. On approx. 90% of trials, participants perform the primary face task: judging whether the two faces have the same or different orientation (both upright, both inverted, or mismatched). This ensures attention is directed away from the faint central Landolt stimulus. On the remaining ~10% of trials, participants instead rate the visibility of the Landolt stimulus on a 4-point PAS scale (1 = no experience, 4 = clear experience). On a subset of PAS-rating trials, no Landolt stimulus is actually presented — yet participants are still asked to rate its visibility. Any non-zero visibility ratings on these "absent" trials index illusory perception driven by expectation under inattention.
The experiment comprises 3 blocks (80 + 96 + 96 = 272 trials) with a break at the midpoint. Two practice blocks (16 trials each, with Landolt stimuli at varying opacity levels) precede the main experiment.

#### Number of participants (raw)
Krakow: 286
#### Conditions and number of trials
272 trials across 3 blocks. 4 face orientation conditions (upright–upright, inverted–inverted, upright–inverted, inverted–upright) × 4 Landolt orientations. ~90% face-judgment trials, ~10% PAS-rating trials (Landolt present or absent). 32 practice trials.

#### Behavioural Measures
* Accuracy (face orientation judgements)
* RT
* PAS (Perceptual Awareness Scale)
