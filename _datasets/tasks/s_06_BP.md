#### Detailed description

The battery comprises five well-established bistable and multistable perception tasks. In four of the five tasks, participants continuously report their dominant percept via button press during 120-second blocks. The fifth task (Stream/Bounce Illusion) uses discrete trials instead. 

Each continuous task is run under two attention conditions — with and without an attentional manipulation — and repeated across two runs, for a total of 6 blocks per session. Block order is counterbalanced across 20 possible orderings. The full battery takes approximately 90 minutes.

1. Motion-Induced Blindness (MIB). A salient target is superimposed on a field of coherently moving distractors. Despite remaining physically present, the target periodically disappears from awareness. Participants report whether the target is visible or invisible.

2. Necker Cube. The classic ambiguous wireframe cube is presented continuously. Participants report which of the two possible 3D orientations they perceive at any given moment.

3. Rubin Face-Vase. The well-known figure–ground ambiguity in which the same contour can be seen as two faces in profile or a central vase. Participants report which interpretation dominates.

4. Auditory Streaming. A repeating sequence of tones at two different frequencies can be perceived either as a single integrated stream or as two segregated streams. Participants report their current percept.

5. Stream/Bounce Illusion. Two visual objects move toward each other and coincide. The event can be perceived as streaming through or bouncing off. Unlike the other tasks, this one uses discrete trials, and reaction time is recorded in addition to the perceptual report.

#### Conditions and number of trials

4 subtasks (MIB, Auditory Streaming, Necker/AF, Rubin) × 2 attention conditions (±attention) × 2 runs each. 
1. Auditory Streaming (522 datasets)
2. Motion-induced blindness (1607 datasets)
3. Necker cube (530 datasets)
4. Rubin Face-Vase (312 datasets)
5. Stream/Bounce Illusion (182 datasets)

#### Behavioural Measures 

* Key presses:
	* Proportion of time with no key (0)
	* Proportion of time with L key (-1)
	* Proportion of time with R key (1)
* * Key switches
* RT (for Stream/Bounce Illusion only)
