#### Detailed description

This procedure comprised two separate binocular rivalry tasks, both presented via a mirror stereoscope (split-screen display mode).
Face–House rivalry. A grayscale face image is presented to one eye and a grayscale house image to the other. Participants continuously report which image dominates their perception by holding down the corresponding arrow key (left or right). Each block lasts 120 seconds, and the assignment of face/house to left/right eye is counterbalanced. The stimulus position is slightly jittered between blocks (up to 10 pixels diagonally) to reduce afterimage effects. There are 2 practice blocks (30 s each) followed by 8 experimental blocks (4 face–house image pairs × 2 eye-position repetitions).
Crossmodal rivalry (flickering gratings). Based on the paradigm of Davidson, Alais, van Boxtel & Tsuchiya (2018). Each eye is presented with a sinusoidally amplitude-modulated gabor grating — one flickering at 4.5 Hz and the other at 20 Hz. Participants continuously report their dominant percept via button press throughout 3-minute blocks. During each block, brief crossmodal cues (auditory tones, tactile vibrations, or both simultaneously) are presented at either 4.5 or 20 Hz, matching or mismatching the dominant flicker. Cues vary in duration (2, 3.1, or 4 s) and are separated by 7–10 s jittered intervals. Three visual-only null periods (2.6 s) per block serve as baseline.
The crossmodal task includes an attention manipulation across two testing days: on one day, participants are instructed to ignore the crossmodal cues; on the other, they attend to the cues and silently count how often the cue frequency matches their dominant percept at cue offset. Day order is counterbalanced. A pre-experiment calibration procedure adjusts contrast to approximately equate dominance durations of the two flicker frequencies for each participant.

#### Number of participants (raw)

Krakow: 260 (Face–House), 292 (Crossmodal)

#### Conditions and number of trials
1. Face–House: 2 practice blocks (30 s) + 8 experimental blocks (120 s each). 4 face–house image pairs × 2 eye positions. Continuous button-press recording.
2. Crossmodal: 24 three-minute blocks per day × 2 days. 3 crossmodal cue types (auditory, tactile, simultaneous) × 2 frequencies (4.5 Hz, 20 Hz). 12 cue trials + 3 visual-only baseline periods per block. 2 attention conditions across days (ignore vs. attend cues). Preceded by dominance-duration calibration.

#### Behavioural Measures
* Keys pressed (dominant percept report, sampled at 60 Hz)
* Key switches
* Accuracy (crossmodal task only)
