#### Detailed description

Participants sat in front of the monitor, laying their right hand under a tilted computer screen (covered by a black cloth) directly beneath the Leap Motion controller and saw their modeled, virtual hand on the monitor. Motion tracking and virtual modeling of the hand was realized by a Leap Motion controller (Leap Motion Inc., San Francisco, CA), creating a virtual-reality model of the hand in real time. Each trial started by presenting (i) a fixation cross, then (ii) the modeled virtual hand (in which the participants conducted a flexion of the index finger and saw it on-screen, either altered or unaltered), followed by (iii) an agency question regarding the movement they conducted and saw (iv), and a confidence rating on a seven-point scale (-3 to 3). Responses were given with a numeric keyboard. There were two types of altered movement, either a delay of the movement on screen or a spatial distortion of the index finger position.

#### Number of participants (raw)

Krakow: 284

#### Conditions and number of trials

Veridical vs distorted movement replay
Two types of movement distortion (temporal/spatial)
Four difficulty levels per type of distortion
240 trials in total.

#### Behavioural Measures 
* Agency judgment (yes/no)
* Confidence rating (7-point scale)
* RT
