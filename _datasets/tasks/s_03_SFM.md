#### Detailed description

Participants have observed an ambiguous rotating sphere of dots presented against a black background continuously for 16 minutes. The sphere comprised 200 full-contrast white dots with a diameter subtending 3.5°, whereas the composition of dots in the sphere was individually randomised in all participants. The dots moved sinusoidally with an angular velocity of 120°/sec. A red fixation cross (.5° in height and width) was superimposed on the centre of the sphere to aid steady fixation. Participants were instructed to report the perceived direction of the rotation of the sphere by pressing and holding down the left or the right arrow key. Only if the motion direction was completely unclear the participant could release both arrow keys. Participants were instructed to try not to influence the perception of dot motion on screen and were monitored throughout the procedure.
